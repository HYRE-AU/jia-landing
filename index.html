What We Found

We spent months listening to families.

We went looking for the truth about what happens to family relationships when distance becomes the default. Reddit. Twitter. App Store reviews. Academic journals.

Parents' tech calls are actually loneliness calls

When mum calls about a popup on her laptop, she doesn't need IT help. She needs to hear your voice. Every small tech problem becomes a legitimate reason to reach out — because there's no other easy, low-pressure way to say "I miss you."

r/AgingParents · 114 upvotes

Having kids makes the distance unbearable

Moving abroad was manageable before children. But once you're a parent, the weight of your own parents not being there — not seeing first steps, not being part of the village — becomes something you carry every single day.

r/expats · 214 upvotes

WhatsApp becomes toxic when it matters most

When aging parents need care, family group chats turn into guilt trips and blame games. Siblings weaponize distance. The tool designed for communication becomes the source of family conflict at the worst possible time.

Leaked family chat · r/Bolehland · 1,050 upvotes

Existing apps are failing on the thing that matters most

We analyzed thousands of App Store reviews for family apps. The #1 complaint: no time zone awareness. Followed closely by: too complicated for parents, feels like just another social media app, and no way to participate without posting content.
